A Fabrication Method Enabling Uniform Expansion for Foaming-driven Shaping and Customizable Stiffness

← Back to Selected Works

Overview

I joined Guanyun Lab as an intern around April 2024, which marked the beginning of my research journey. Here, I delved into various fields such as material-driven design, soft robotics, and 4D printing. During this academic year, I contributed as the third author to a project titled GyFoam.

GyFoam is a novel fabrication method that integrates foam material with lattice structures to achieve controlled and uniform expansion. This method enables high-quality forming in appearance and customizable stiffness in function, utilizing standard 3D printers, filaments, commercially available Thermo-Expandable Microspheres, and silicone. To achieve customizable stiffness, we explored two methods through experimentation: modifying material concentration and adjusting lattice structural parameters. Additionally, we proposed three shape control strategies for creating complex shapes: bending, wavy edges, and internal doming.

We also developed a user-friendly design tool that allows users to construct lattice structures, preview basic deformations, and generate mold models for printing. Through a series of applications, we demonstrated GyFoam's practical usage in fabricating large objects, wearable products, enabling flexible interactions, and creating aesthetic designs.

Morphing

Bilayer-induced Bending
This deformation strategy utilizes differential expansion between two layers: upon heat activation, the higher-concentration layer undergoes greater expansion, serving as the driving layer, while the lower-concentration layer acts as a restricting layer, resisting deformation and generating controlled bending moments. Our deformation library illustrates how the concentration of the driving layer influences the degree of bending.

Concentric-induced Wavy Edges
We present a structure in which the inner ring has a lower composite concentration, while the outer ring has a higher concentration. During expansion, the outer ring is constrained and stretched by the inner ring, resulting in periodic wavy edges. By adjusting the composite concentrations and geometric dimensions, we can achieve wavy edges with varying amplitudes and wave counts.

Concentric-induced Internal Doming
In contrast to wavy edges, internal doming is achieved by reversing the concentration distribution. The inner region, with its higher expansion capability, is constrained by the surrounding structure, preventing lateral expansion and instead forcing upward deformation into a vertical dome shape. Our library showcases various internal doming effects resulting from different concentration gradients, illustrating a diverse range of dome heights and shapes.

Project Significance

The GyFoam project addresses several critical challenges in the use of expandable foam materials for interactive and personalized fabrication:

Solving uneven expansion issues: Traditional thermo-expandable foams often suffer from non-uniform expansion due to poor heat distribution, especially in large or complex geometries. By integrating foam with gyroid lattice structures, GyFoam ensures controlled and uniform expansion, significantly improving shape fidelity and structural quality.

Enabling customizable stiffness for personalization: The combination of foaming materials and lattice architectures allows independent tuning of mechanical stiffness by adjusting material concentration and structural parameters. This capability unlocks new opportunities for personalized products tailored to individual ergonomic and functional needs.

Bridging material science and interaction design: Beyond mechanical optimization, GyFoam expands the design space of soft fabrication by enabling programmable shape morphing, scalable manufacturing, and multi-functional applications — from large-scale furniture to wearable devices and interactive interfaces. This integration empowers designers and makers to prototype soft, adaptive, and responsive physical systems with greater control and creativity.
