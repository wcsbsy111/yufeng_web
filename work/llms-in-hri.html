A Systematic Review of LLM-driven Impacts in HRI Research

← Back to Selected Works

Overview

This review examines the emergence of Large Language Models (LLMs) in Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) and synthesizes current work to identify mechanisms, methods, applications, and challenges. We summarize the state of the art and provide actionable directions for future research in LLM-empowered HRI.

Background

Since 2021, there has been a significant surge in research focusing on the integration of Large Language Models (LLMs) in Human-Robot Interaction (HRI). This upward trend reflects the growing recognition of LLMs' potential to revolutionize how humans interact with robots, offering more natural and intuitive interfaces.

However, the current landscape of research is fragmented, with studies scattered across various aspects of LLM application in HRI. This lack of cohesion can make it challenging to consolidate emerging challenges and opportunities effectively. The field is in need of systematic reviews and analyses that can synthesize the diverse research methodologies and evaluation strategies being employed.

Research Questions

This review is guided by the following research questions:

  1. How do LLMs transform the HRI research domain? (RQ1)
  2. How are LLMs integrated into HRI systems? (RQ2)
  3. What research methods are used to assess LLM-driven HRI systems? (RQ3)

Upon reviewing the literature, we further explored the following research questions:

  1. What new opportunities and challenges do LLMs introduce to HRI, and how to navigate potential trade-offs? (RQ4)
  2. How are the technical and evaluation requirements for LLM–HRI systems dependent on specific scenarios and user groups, and what implications can be derived for future LLM-driven HRI research? (RQ5)

Analysis

This section systematically analyzes the profound impact of large language models (LLMs) on human-robot interaction (HRI), covering three layers: interaction mechanisms, research methods and evaluation strategies, and key application domains.

Reshaping Interaction Mechanisms

LLMs are fundamentally transforming how humans interact with robots, endowing them with enhanced contextual understanding, natural communication, and adaptive capabilities.

Context-Aware Interaction

Robots can integrate environmental perception and user state information to achieve dual understanding of both the scene and the person, supporting personalized dialogue, alignment with social norms, and interaction repair.

Natural Interaction and Collaboration

By inferring implicit intentions, recognizing emotional states, and proactively establishing shared understanding, robots evolve from passive executors to collaborative partners, capable of joint task planning, initiating social engagement, and maintaining trust through explanations and apologies.

In-situ Learning

LLMs enable robots to continuously learn and adapt from real-time feedback, constructing dynamic user models that enhance trust and acceptance in long-term interactions.

Research Methods and Evaluation Strategies

Current HRI research emphasizes human-centered evaluation frameworks, combining quantitative and qualitative methods to assess LLM-driven system performance.

Research Methods: These include lab experiments, real-world deployments, interviews, surveys, and technical assessments, often supplemented with Wizard-of-Oz setups and co-design workshops to manage model uncertainty.

Evaluation Metrics:

Objective metrics: task efficiency, accuracy, response latency, model prediction performance.

Subjective metrics: user satisfaction, perceived intelligence, anthropomorphism, usability, safety, and cognitive load, highlighting interaction quality and relational experience.

Expansion of Application Domains

The integration of LLMs is driving HRI deployment and deepening across multiple scenarios, including:

  • Social and Conversational Systems: e.g., open-domain dialogue, personality modeling
  • Healthcare and Wellbeing: e.g., psychological counseling, rehabilitation assistance
  • Daily Life and Home Assistance: e.g., cleaning, cooking, emotional companionship
  • Education and Learning: e.g., co-creative storytelling, language learning
  • Emerging Domains: industrial manufacturing, AR/VR interaction, public space services, and more

Discussion

Technical Limitations: This paper analyzes the technical challenges faced by large language models (LLMs) when applied to human-robot interaction (HRI), including latency, inconsistency, lack of transparency, and unpredictability. It also discusses how these limitations affect the effectiveness and reliability of LLMs in HRI systems.

Emotional Intelligence: This paper explores the progress and current capabilities of LLMs for implementing emotional intelligence in robots, including real-time modeling of users' emotional states, personalized interactions, and context-based dialogue adjustments. It also discusses the challenges of multimodal perception and how LLMs can achieve more stable and adaptable social cognition.

Long-Term Interaction Focus: This paper highlights the potential issues associated with long-term interactions with LLM-powered robots, including users' emotional attachment to robots, the long-term maintenance of personalized experiences, and how to manage these long-term interactions through transparency, privacy protection, and fairness.

Ethical Considerations: This paper discusses the ethical challenges introduced by integrating LLMs into robots, especially when deployed in sensitive environments such as with children, the elderly, and students. It explores ethical issues in trust, privacy, security, and multi-user interactions, as well as responsible emotional intelligence in long-term interactions.
