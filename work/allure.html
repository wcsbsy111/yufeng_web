Design Accessibility - Visual Impairment

← Back to Selected Works

Overview

This project marked my first experience into accessibility design within a multicultural environment. The experience was not only enjoyable but also profoundly enlightening, offering a unique perspective on the importance of inclusive design practices.

The project focused on developing a comprehensive solution to enhance the fashion and beauty experience for visually impaired individuals aged 18 to 30. Our aim was to empower this demographic with the confidence and tools necessary to maintain a well-groomed appearance and integrate seamlessly into society. We designed "Allure," a brand that embodies simplicity, elegance, and customization, with a key focus on providing real-time fashion assistance and accessible information on self-care and beauty. The solution includes a high-performance camera system integrated into an earphone device, which offers audio advice for makeup application, a timer for skincare routines, and AI recognition for navigation and object identification. Additionally, we developed an accompanying application that assists with makeup coordination, clothing selection, and outdoor navigation, ensuring that users can manage their personal grooming tasks independently and with confidence.

Background

visual impairments can be hindered by inadequate design, lack of alternative information formats, insufficient assistive technology, and public awareness. The aim for technology is to provide easy access to critical information and boost self-confidence for visually impaired individuals, aiming to support young visually impaired individuals and their families.
