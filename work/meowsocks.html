Overview

This is my first venture into commercializing a design project, transforming concepts into reality with immense satisfaction. The initiative centered on launching a pop-up store, "Socks with Cats," which aimed to merge fashion and playfulness to create a unique shopping experience.

Key activities included conducting thorough market research to understand the Chinese sock market's dynamics, consumer preferences, and competitive strategies. The project involved strategic product selection, pricing, and promotional activities to engage our target audience effectively.

The design was brought to life through a vibrant color scheme and a mascot, "Miao," enhancing brand identity. The execution encompassed on-site setup, daily operations, and adaptive strategies based on customer feedback, culminating in a successful commercial and creative endeavor.

Traffic Analysis

The peak foot traffic in the food court occurs from 11:30 to 12:30, during the lunch rush. There is a smaller evening peak, but it is much lower than at noon.

We estimated the foot traffic around the pop-up store for 30 minutes from 11:30 to 12:00 and recorded the number of visitors entering the store and making purchases.

  • Total foot traffic: 686 people
  • Estimated male/female split: 429 men, 257 women
  • Visitors entering the store: 35
  • Successful sale: 1 transaction of 20 pairs

Note: Although foot traffic is highest during this period, customers tend to purchase after finishing their meals, so the purchase rate is relatively low at this time. The peak purchasing period is around 12:30–13:30, when foot traffic is lower but almost every visitor entering the store makes a purchase. Due to the scattered nature of this crowd, precise statistics for this period are not meaningful.
