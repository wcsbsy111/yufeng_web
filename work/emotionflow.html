Overview

In Chinese, we often use the word “倾诉” — to speak out and share our emotions — and yet we also say emotions need an “出口,” a way to be poured out and released. EmotionFlow is built upon this metaphor, merging the acts of speaking and pouring into one seamless, embodied experience. It transforms the abstract process of emotional expression into a tangible ritual: users speak their feelings softly into the bottle, watching them gradually accumulate as rising “ink” — a visual metaphor for being heard. When ready, they gently tilt the bottle, allowing the ink to flow out, symbolizing emotional release and renewal. Through this integration of language, visualization, and physical action, EmotionFlow offers a quiet, intimate space for self-regulation — where emotions are not only expressed, but also seen, contained, and finally set free.

Background

In contemporary society, young people are facing an unprecedented dilemma of “emotional isolation.” Many of us struggle to voice certain feelings, which linger and accumulate deep inside without release. According to our survey, 41.2% of respondents said they “often feel anxious or emotionally low,” while 70% believe that current ways of emotional expression are “limited and ineffective.”

Behind this phenomenon lies a combination of multiple factors — mounting social pressures and a growing imbalance in emotional expression mechanisms:

High-pressure living environment: The relentless competition in study, work, and life keeps individuals under prolonged emotional stress, creating an urgent need for a safe and effective outlet.

Challenges in emotional expression: Although the information age has improved communication efficiency, empathic capacity has declined. Fragmented expression on social media struggles to carry complex emotions, often leading to situations where “voices go unheard” and “feelings are misunderstood.”

However, many traditional emotional release methods have inherent limitations:

High barriers to psychological counseling: Professional resources are scarce and expensive, and social stigma remains a significant deterrent.

Risks in social sharing: Some emotions are difficult to express openly, and concerns about privacy leaks often suppress genuine expression.

Lack of feedback in journaling: One-way writing lacks interactivity, making it hard to form a complete emotional processing loop.

In response, we aim to combine scientific principles, artistic expression, and human-centered care to offer young people a new kind of emotional outlet — one that is both functional and ritualistic. It allows users to “speak their stories, even if their voices tremble,” and fills a crucial gap in the current market for emotional externalization and creative release.

Function Design

Confession Entry: Recording starts when the button is pressed, and the microphone captures the user’s voice.

Progress Visualization: The E-Ink display renders a real-time waveform of the recorded audio.

Data Upload: The voice data is sent to a local server for processing and analysis.

Emotional Release Ritual: Tilt gesture recognition (via MPU6050) allows the user to “pour out” their sadness through a symbolic motion.

Usage Scenarios

In the quiet nights spent alone in the dorm, during stressful afternoons filled with coursework, or in those low moments when there’s no one to talk to, EmotionFlow becomes your most gentle and silent listener.

Simply speak softly into the bottle’s opening — the words you can’t say out loud and the emotions that go unheard are gently stored as rising ink inside the bottle. This visual response provides a sense of being heard and understood.

When you’re ready to let go, tilt the bottle slowly onto the screen. As the ink drips down, ripples and swimming fish appear, symbolizing the release and rebirth of your emotions. This is more than just expression — it’s a metaphorical ritual of healing.

Whether as a daily self-regulation habit, a moment of reflection before sleep, or a tool for emotional management, EmotionFlow offers a quiet, uninterrupted way to express yourself — heard through words, comforted through visuals, and released through motion.
